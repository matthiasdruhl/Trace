# Trace primary persona

Last updated: 2026-04-24

This document defines the primary user persona for Trace.

The goal is not to support every possible archive-search user. The goal is to
anchor the README, demo, UI, and pitch around one clear investigation workflow
that makes the value of Trace obvious fast.

## Primary persona

- Name: Maya Chen
- Role: Senior Trust & Safety Investigator
- Company type: mobility or marketplace platform with archived incident and compliance records
- Team: Trust & Safety, Compliance Operations, or Regulatory Response
- Experience level: mid-career operator with enough context to run investigations, but not interested in search infrastructure for its own sake

## Top-level framing

For the demo and dataset, Maya remains the concrete anchor persona.

For submission materials, Trace should be framed more broadly as a tool for a
regulatory, compliance, or trust and safety investigator handling high-stakes
historical cases across messy archives.

## One-line summary

Maya investigates historical safety, insurance, permit, and compliance issues
across a large archive of records and needs to find, explain, and hand off the
right evidence quickly even when the records do not use the same wording as the
case she is working.

## Working environment

Maya works in a platform business that operates across many cities and
jurisdictions. Records come from different teams, different workflows, and
different reporting styles.

The archive includes:

- safety incident logs
- insurance lapse reports
- vehicle inspection audits
- city permit renewals
- driver background flags
- privacy and compliance records

The wording is inconsistent, but the operational stakes are real.

## Main responsibilities

- investigate archived incidents and escalations
- respond to regulator, legal, or internal audit requests
- find prior related cases and supporting evidence
- narrow evidence to the right jurisdiction, date range, and document category
- prepare defensible findings for other teams

## Core goals

- find the right archived records quickly
- avoid missing relevant evidence because exact keywords differ
- reduce noisy result sets to the specific operational context that matters
- produce trustworthy, defensible evidence for decisions
- generate a clean handoff for compliance, legal, or leadership

## Main pain points

- keyword search misses semantically related records
- broad semantic search alone can be too noisy for real investigation work
- records are written by different teams with inconsistent terminology
- cross-city and cross-document searches create distracting near-misses
- manual archive review is slow and hard to trust under time pressure

## Jobs to be done

- "Help me find related archived incidents when exact words do not match."
- "Help me narrow the search to the city, document type, and time window that matter for this case."
- "Help me trust that I did not miss the one record that changes the investigation."
- "Help me hand off a defensible set of results to compliance, legal, or leadership."

## What Maya values

- speed
- confidence
- defensibility
- precision
- low cognitive load

She does not want a generic search box that returns interesting text. She wants
an investigation tool that helps her find the right evidence, explain why it
belongs in the case, and hand that case forward with confidence.

## Product promise for this persona

Trace helps trust and safety and compliance investigators turn messy archive
search into a defensible investigation workflow by combining semantic retrieval,
structured filters, and explainable evidence handoff.

## Why Trace fits this persona

Trace is valuable to Maya because it combines three things she needs at the
same time:

- semantic flexibility to find records that describe the same issue using different language
- structured control to narrow results by city, document type, and time
- explainable output that can support a handoff, escalation, or audit response

Keyword search alone is too brittle. Broad semantic search alone is too loose.
Trace is designed for the operational middle ground between flexible discovery
and defensible action.

## Submission-ready persona paragraph

Trace is built for regulatory, compliance, and trust and safety investigators
who need to review large archives of historical incident records.
These investigators often know the kind of case they are looking for, but not
the exact language used in prior reports. Keyword search can miss relevant
evidence, while broad semantic search alone can be too noisy for real
operational use. Trace solves this by combining semantic retrieval with
structured metadata filters like city, document type, and time, then packaging
the retrieved evidence into a more explainable investigation workflow that
helps teams respond faster and with more confidence.
